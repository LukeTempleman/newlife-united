About Newlife United

Ordinary people,
extraordinary God.

A church planted in Germiston in 1987, sent to know Jesus and make Him known — across the city, across the continent, across the nations.

§ 01 — Our story
EST. 1987 · GERMISTON

From a living room
to a house of nations.

Newlife United was planted in 1987 with a handful of believers and an audacious prayer: that Germiston would become a launchpad for revival into the nations. Nearly four decades later, that prayer is still our compass.

We are an ordinary church believing for a supernatural God to do the impossible — in homes, in workplaces, in prisons, across borders. What He did in scripture, He is still doing now.

"My house shall be called a house of prayer for all nations."

Your Next Step · Growth Track

Four steps
one journey.

Growth Track takes you from first steps with Jesus to a life of purpose and ministry — one step at a time, at your own pace.

THE track.

01
STEP 1

KNOW GOD

The first step is knowing God personally — not just knowing about Him. Our services and Discover Newlife sessions will introduce you to the heart and mission of Newlife United.

STEP 2

FIND FREEDOM

Join a Small Group! We want people to gather together based on things they enjoy and are important to them. Essentially, the things you are already doing in your life are the things that can make up your Small Group. Find Freedom as you walk together, support and disciple each other on your journey with God.

02
03
STEP 3

DISCOVER PURPOSE

We believe every person has dreams and gifts that God has placed within them and want to see those gifts developed by being used to further God's kingdom. It's only when you discover your God-given purpose that you find joy in being who God has created you to be. Growth Track will enable you to find your God-given purpose.

STEP 4

MAKE A DIFFERENCE

Join the Dream Team! All of us were created by God to make a difference in the world around us. God has a place for you where your unique abilities and passions can impact the lives of others. In the Dream Team you will begin to serve others with your gifts, bringing your life purpose.

Equipping Courses

THE courses.

Short, term-based. Come for one course or work through the whole track — at your own pace, in your own season.

01
Growth Track

Know God, Find Freedom, Discover Purpose, Make a Difference — the four-step discipleship journey every member is invited to take.

Learn more →
02
Unite It

A foundational teaching on the local church — what it is, why it matters, and how every member is a vital part of the body.

03
Finance Course

Biblical stewardship, practical budgeting, debt freedom, and the theology of generosity. Taught by people who've walked it out.

04
Alpha Course

The classic introduction to the Christian faith — open to anyone with questions. A safe space to explore, doubt, and discover.

05
Share Your Faith

Practical and theological — how to tell your story, start conversations, and give a reason for the hope inside you.

06
Everyday Evangelism

Street-level and workplace-level. How to live a life that makes people ask questions — and how to answer them when they do.

07
Mentoring for Multiplication

The discipleship model Jesus used. How to pour into someone else's life so that they pour into others — two by two, not one by one.

08
Gifts of the Spirit

Biblical, charismatic, and practical. What the gifts are, how they operate, and how to move in them without getting weird about it.

09
Fruit of the Spirit

Character formation rooted in Galatians 5. Love, joy, peace — and how the Spirit grows them in you as you yield rather than perform.

10
Prophetic Equipping

Hearing God's voice, testing what you hear, and delivering a word that builds rather than burdens. Grounded in Scripture and pastoral accountability.

11
The Big Picture

The whole Bible in one overview — how it fits together, how every book points to Christ, and how the story ends. Recommended for every believer.

12
The Tool Kit

Practical disciplines: prayer, Bible study, fasting, worship, and giving. The habits that sustain a life with God when the feelings aren't there.

13
Healing the Sick

Biblical mandate and practical activation. Praying for the sick with confidence — in your home, your workplace, and on the streets.

14
Fix It

Practical men's skills — maintenance, repairs, and the things your dad should have taught you. Brotherhood, laughter, and hands-on work.

15
Build It

For those called to lead — a course on building teams, raising up disciples, and planting something that outlasts you.

Care Ministries

CARE that
walks with you.

Life gets hard. These aren't programmes — they're communities of people who have walked through the same valley and know the way out.

Divorce Care

A safe, confidential space for anyone experiencing separation or divorce. Practical support, grief processing, and the hope of a future.

Grief Share

For anyone who has lost a loved one. A community that honours grief rather than rushing past it, and points gently toward hope.

Wholeness Course

Inner healing, identity, and freedom from the wounds that follow us into adulthood. Facilitated by trained pastoral counsellors.

Parenting Course

Raising children is the hardest thing you'll ever do. Practical tools, Biblical principles, and a room full of parents who get it.

Single & Parenting

Doing it alone is never God's plan. This community of single parents holds each other up, shares the load, and finds joy in the middle of hard.

Safety first

Safe and
known.

Your child's safety is not an afterthought — it is the first thing we get right.

01
Background-checked volunteers
Every adult working with children in Kids For Christ has completed a police clearance check and our internal Safe Church training.
02
Check-in system
Each child gets a unique label on drop-off. You keep the matching stub — no stub, no collection. It's simple and it works.
03
Two adults per room, always
No child is ever alone with a single adult. Two volunteers are present at all times in every room.
04
No pressure drop-off
Babies and toddlers are completely welcome in the main service. You decide what works for your family — we follow your lead.

The idea

What a
group is.

We believe that the things you enjoy and care about are the very things that can make up your Small Group. You don't need a new life to join one — you bring the one you have.

Groups meet in homes, coffee shops, parks, and living rooms. They cook together, study Scripture together, carry each other's weight, and pray for each other by name. This is discipleship the way Jesus did it — in the ordinary rhythms of life.
